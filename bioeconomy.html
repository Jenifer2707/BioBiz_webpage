India’s Bioeconomy – Sectors, Highlights & Companies

Bioeconomy

INTRODUCTION

The bioeconomy represents a sustainable and innovative framework that utilizes biological resources, knowledge, and technologies to address critical global challenges and drive economic growth. It integrates advancements in biotechnology, agriculture, healthcare, environmental conservation, and many industrial processes to create value while minimizing environmental impact.

By leveraging renewable biological resources and reducing dependency on fossil fuels, the bioeconomy fosters sustainable development, enhances resource efficiency, and supports climate change mitigation. Current advancements include breakthroughs in genetic engineering, precision agriculture, biopharmaceuticals, and biodegradable materials, which are already transforming industries and improving quality of life.

At the same time, efforts in biodiversity conservation and bioremediation are crucial for maintaining ecological balance and restoring degraded environments. As innovation accelerates, the bioeconomy holds immense potential for reshaping traditional sectors, creating new markets, and promoting a circular economy. With strategic investments in research, infrastructure, and policy frameworks, it offers a pathway to a resilient, inclusive, and low-carbon future.

HIGHLIGHTS

  • National Bioenergy Programme Launched by the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE) for 2021-26, this program supports the development of biogas, biomass, and waste-to-energy projects, with an initial budget of ₹858 crore.
  • India has installed over 10 GW of biomass-based power, largely driven by bagasse cogeneration in sugar mills. These projects harness agricultural by-products for power generation, especially in sugarcane-producing states like Uttar Pradesh and Maharashtra​
  • Sustainable Alternative Towards Affordable Transportation (SATAT) This initiative encourages the production of Compressed Bio Gas (CBG) from agricultural residues, municipal solid waste, and other biomass sources, aiming to establish 5,000 CBG plants by 2025.
  • The government has expedited ethanol blending in petrol, achieving a 10% blend rate in 2022 and targeting 20% by 2025, reducing oil imports and supporting the sugar industry.
  • Incentives are provided for setting up biomass pellet and briquette manufacturing plants, with subsidies up to ₹45 lakh per plant, to supply clean fuel to industries and power plants

The program has helped protect critical species like the black-eyed crane and promote sustainable tourism.
  • Through initiatives like the Green India Mission, India has committed to an investment of $6.5 billion over 5 years to conserve biodiversity and restore ecosystems, with a focus on rural areas.
